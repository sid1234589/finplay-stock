About Game Profile

Level One

We know that

The stock market operates like a multiplayer game where players trade shares (ownership pieces of companies). Prices shift based on real-world factors like company news, investor emotions, or global events - similar to how rare in-game items gain or lose value.


To play, you use a broker (your "game interface") to place orders, which the stock exchange matches with other traders, moving shares into your secure "inventory" (Demat account). This blend of strategy, timing, and real-world dynamics makes it a high-stakes, ever-changing marketplace.

SO... What Happens After You Buy?
(Holding Your Items)

You keep the shares, hoping the company does well and the share price goes up.

OR

Some companies share a small part of their profits with their shareholders. This is like getting bonus points or rewards for holding an item (Dividends).

OR

If the price goes up, you can sell your shares to another player for a profit.

What's your Game Plan?

Short-Term

Buys and sells shares relatively quickly (holding for less than a year).

V / S

Long-Term

Buys shares and holds them for over a year, believing in the company's future growth. (Often, tax benefits for playing the long game).

Are you a long-term strategist, patiently holding shares? Or a quick-moving trader looking for daily or weekly wins? Decide based on your goals and how much risk you're comfortable taking.
